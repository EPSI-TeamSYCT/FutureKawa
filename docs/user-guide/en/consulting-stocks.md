# Consulting Stocks

This page explains how to browse your coffee stock: choosing a country and
warehouse, reading the FIFO lot list, and opening a lot.

## 1. Choose a country and warehouse

Use the country filter at the top of the app to pick **Brazil**, **Ecuador** or
**Colombia**. You can then narrow down to a single **warehouse** within that
country. The **Warehouses** screen shows each warehouse of the selected country
with its latest measured temperature and humidity, so you can see at a glance
which sites are healthy and which are drifting.

## 2. Read the FIFO lot list

Open the **Lots** screen to see all coffee lots. Lots are always sorted
**FIFO — First In, First Out**, meaning the **oldest lot in storage appears
first**. This is the order in which coffee should normally leave the warehouse, so
the top of the list is what needs your attention first.

For each lot the list shows:

- an identifier for the lot;
- the warehouse and country where it is stored;
- the **storage date** and how many **days** it has been in stock;
- a **status** indicator (see below).

### Understanding lot status

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **OK / healthy** | Conditions are within the ideal band and the lot is not too old. |
| **Out of range** | Temperature or humidity is outside the country's tolerance band. |
| **Too old** | The lot has been in storage for more than 365 days. |

You can filter the list by country, by warehouse and by status to focus, for
example, only on lots that need action.

## 3. Open a lot

Click a lot in the list to open its **detail page**. There you will find:

- the lot's key information (warehouse, country, storage date, age);
- its **temperature and humidity curves** over time;
- the ideal band for the country, drawn on the chart.

Reading these curves is covered in [Reading the charts](reading-charts.md).
