# Understanding Alerts

Alerts are how FutureKawa tells you that something needs attention. This page
explains the two kinds of alert and what to do about each.

## Where to find alerts

- The **Dashboard** shows the most recent alerts across all countries.
- The **Alerts** screen lists every active alert. You can filter it by country and
  hide alerts that have already been handled.

## The two kinds of alert

### 1. Out-of-range conditions

The temperature or humidity of a warehouse has drifted **outside the country's
tolerance band** and stayed there. The coffee stored on that site is at risk of
losing quality.

**What to do:**

1. Open the warehouse or lot to see the curve and confirm the drift is sustained,
   not a single spike (see [Reading the charts](reading-charts.md)).
2. Check the physical storage: ventilation, doors, air conditioning or heating,
   and any humidity source.
3. Bring the conditions back inside the band.
4. Once the situation is under control, **mark the alert as handled** so it leaves
   the active list.

### 2. Lot too old

A lot has been in storage for **more than 365 days**. Older green coffee gradually
loses value, so it should be prioritised for shipment.

**What to do:**

1. Open the **Lots** list — the oldest lots are already at the top (FIFO).
2. Prioritise the flagged lot for the next outbound shipment.
3. Coordinate with the warehouse and headquarters as needed.

## Marking an alert as handled

Once you have dealt with an alert, mark it as **handled**. It then disappears from
the active list, which keeps the screen focused on problems that still need action.
Handled alerts can still be reviewed by removing the filter.
